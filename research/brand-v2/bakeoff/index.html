What this is

A bake-off for the Citizen Mason V2 landing page. The current live site scores 2 of 5 on distinctiveness and 2 of 5 on memorability. That is the floor to escape. Every direction below aims at owned, recognizable-on-sight design with operator-built warmth, calm authority over hype, and one dominant ask.

PAIN 01

Live site: 2 / 5 distinctiveness
PAIN 03

02

Wordmark and mark

Six owned marks on one sheet, each tested at lockup size and at a true 32px favicon. The mark must survive a thumbnail and sell the name without a caption.

Wordmark sheet, six marks Recommended: The Quoin
The Quoin, dressed corner masonry
A stacked-stone corner. The favicon sheds the faint infill and renders the bold L of three stones, instantly recognizable at 32px. Solid fills, so it inverts trivially on dark and in one-color print.
Why it wins Top-tier on every axis at once. Architectural and ownable where a rounded-square CM monogram (Maker's Stamp, the score-84 runner-up) is the default app-icon pattern. A quoin is dressed corner masonry, so the mark sells the name with no caption. Runner-up to keep in the final two: Maker's Stamp CM, the safe-legibility hedge.
