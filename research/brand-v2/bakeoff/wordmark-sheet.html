Wordmark and mark sheet

Six owned marks for Citizen Mason, built to survive a thumbnail.

The name carries the work: masonry, building, civic trust, a thing made by hand. Each concept below is drawn from that root and shown four ways, so the test is the real one. A mark earns the brand when it reads at 32 pixels, holds on a dark ground, and stands beside the words without leaning on them.

The Quoin
Pairs with: Unfussy Premium

A. Full lockup Citizen Mason
B. Nav size

A quoin is the dressed corner stone that carries the load where two walls meet. The mark interlocks two block sizes into a corner, full weight for the structural course, half weight for the infill. At favicon scale it reduces to a clean L of three stones. Geometric, premium, unfussy.

The keystone seated at the crown of a full arch, where the two words meet and the structure holds. It carries the most literal story of the six, an arch stands because the last stone locks it. The serif lockup reads steady and trusted, the reliable proxy who reads the details for you.

Course & Bond
Pairs with: Craftsperson's Ledger / Studio Stamp
06
A. Full lockup Citizen Mason
B. Nav size
C. 32px favicon
D. Reversed

Running-bond coursing, the staggered joint that lets a wall carry weight no single stone could. Each course offsets the one below it. The pattern scales into a section divider, a loading state, and a hero motif, so the brand has a system, not one lonely glyph. The richest as a kit, the busiest at 32px.

Every mark is drawn as inline SVG on currentColor, so it inverts cleanly between light and dark and carries no external asset. The favicon column shows a true 32px tile beside a 2x echo, so the thumbnail decision is made on the real size, not a scaled-up preview. Color is held neutral here on purpose. The mark decision comes first; the owned charcoal and amber gamut lands on the chosen direction in the identity sheets.
